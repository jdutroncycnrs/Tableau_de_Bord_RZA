We focused on 400 m² circular plots (corresponding to an 11.3-m radius) around each of our central temperature sensors. Field data was recorded by up to 4 observers, including at least one botanist. We recorded species presence and abundance in different vegetation layers, or strata, separately: tree, higher and lower shrub layers, as well as the herbaceous and moss layers. Here are the names and definition of each layer:
- m for mosses (bryophytes),
- h for herbaceaous and woody plants lower than 50 cm,
- ab for lower shrubs : "arbustive basse", woody plants from 50 cm to 2 m,
- ah for higher shrubs : "arbustive haute", from 2 to 7 m,
- a for trees : "arborée", more than 7 m high.

In the herbaceous layer, we encompassed herbaceous and woody plants with a maximum height of 50 cm, including saplings. In the moss layer, we encompassed all terricolous and saxicolous (i.e. species growing on the ground or on rocks) bryophyte species.

We recorded whenever a given species occurred solely on a local heterogeneity of the vegetation plot  either on a rock, a humid area (ditch, stream, or puddle) or an area of compacted ground (like skid trails and silvicultural paths, ancient or in activity). 

The abundance-dominance coefficients for vascular plants are coefficients from Braun-Blanquet (1928), adapted by Barkman et al. (1964).  :
- coefficient "2" : Below 5% of cover on the total area of 400 m², 
- coefficient "i" : a unique individual, 
- coefficient "+" : less than 10 individuals, 
- coefficient "1" : more than 10 individuals,
- coefficient "2m" : many individuals; 
then it only depends on the cumulated cover of the species: 2a for 5-15%, 2b for 15-25%, 3 for 25-50%, 4 for 50-70%, and 5 for 75-100% of cover

For bryophytes, abundance coefficients were not attributed. Bryophytes were systematically sampled in paper envelopes, with one specimen per species and per plot, to verify identifications in the laboratory. Our initial identifications were systematically checked by Alain Vanderpoorten, an expert bryologist from Belgium.
