The different datasets available in that collection contain (i) biodiversity surveys (vascular plants, bryophytes and soil artropods), (ii) in-situ microclimate records (both air and soil temperature near the ground surface), (iii) ground-truth measurements of stand characteristics (maximum height, stem diameter, species identity and status) and (iv) high-resolution airborne LiDAR images, all collected across three deciduous state forests in France (Aigoual, Blois and Mormal). (#3) one set for air and soil temperature records; (#4) one set for forest stand characteristics; and (#5) one set for remote sensing data acquired from airborne LiDAR. Each of these 5 different sets are then divided into 3 subsets: one per forest (Aigoual, Blois and Mormal). Within each forest, a total of 60 plots were initially installed at the onset of the project in 2020. Within a given forest, all 60 plots were placed according to a gradient of stand conditions, ranging from very open stand conditions in young forest stages to very dense and closed-canopy conditions in mature forest stands. Within each plot, we installed microclimate sensors (HOBO loggers) to record air and soil temperature in the understory (set #3). We performed a vegetation survey (vascular plants and bryophytes) (set #1), we installed pitfall traps to survey soil arthropods (set #2) and we described stand characteristics (set #4). In addition to this, in summer 2021, we subcontracted a company for airborne LiDAR data acquisition over each forest (set #5).
