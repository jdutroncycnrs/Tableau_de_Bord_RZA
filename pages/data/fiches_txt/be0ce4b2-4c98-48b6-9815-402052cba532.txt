Coordinates are provided in two spatial reference systems : in the French map projection Lambert 93 (EPSG 2154) and in the world spatial reference system, WGS84 (EPSG 4326)

The category of topography can be one of: Down slope, Gentle slope, Level area, Mid slope, Plateau, Ravine, Slight depression, Summit, Up slope

Elevation and associated variables are extracted and calculated from the RGE ALTI 5m, IGN on GIS software (ArcGis Pro 3.2 - ESRI) : we used spatial analysis tools : slope, aspect, TWI
Northness is calculated as the cosinus of aspect, and Northness_slope is the product of northness and slope.

The distance to the nearest forest edge and the distance to the nearest water course are also calculated using GIS software (ArcGis Pro 3.2 - ESRI). Forest edges are extracted from BD FORET v.2, IGN and water is extracted from BD TOPO, IGN.

The percentage of dead wood, rocks or silvicultural paths are visually estimated in the field, respectively as the percentage of projected surface of woody debris (any size), rocks (any size) or any kinds of sylvicultural paths (old or in activity), covering a circular 400 m² area around each central sensor (i.e. a 11.3 m radius). Two overlapping dead branches or rocks count a one. 

Stand structure is determined from field observations. It can be one of: Coppice, Irregular, Isolated trees, Open, Regular. Stand stage can be either the same as stand structure, or in case of a "Regular" structure, one of: Thicket (fourré), Saplings (gaulis), Pole stand (perchis bas et haut), Young stand, Adult stand, Old stand, Seed trees (semenciers).

The dendrometric (tree) protocol used on the plot is either relascope or stem counting, or open if no dendrometric protocol were applied. See "tree suvey" metadata for more information on tree survey protocols.

The pH is measured on the first 0-10 cm layer of soil (below the litter), sampled on two places on each plot (about 5 m North and 5 m South of the central sensor), and then this value is the mean of at least two independent measurements made with a pHmeter.
