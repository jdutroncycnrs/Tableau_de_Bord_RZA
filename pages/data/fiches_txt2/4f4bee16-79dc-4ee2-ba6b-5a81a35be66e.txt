Air sensors, HOBO 64K (64 Ko memory sensors), were attached on the North side of a tree (or wooden pole for open areas), by spring wires to adjust to tree thickness growth. They were placed in white PVC tubes to minimize overheating due to direct sun radiations. The 1-m height for air sensors minimizes game damage risks (especially from wild boars). Air temperature is recorded every hour.
Similarly, soil sensors, HOBO 8K (8 Ko memory sensors), were attached by a wire to the same tree as above (representing the center of the plot) to avoid losses resulting from wildlife activity. Soil temperature is recorded every two hours.

There are gaps in some series of temperature data, due to several reasons: sensor failure, disappearance of the sensor, forest thinning or harvesting.
After one year of installation the data was recorded and measurements re-launched, which made it possible to replace defective or lost sensors.
